CSS specificity determines the order of importance of a CSS declaration. The more specific the declaration, the higher value the declaration is assigned. Declarations with the highest values are the rules that are applied to the HTML. This paragraph is inside a div.

I am a paragraph that is outside of a div. When I'm selected, I will not be as specific as my paragraph friend above. Any declarations made to p will affect me, but any declarations made to a p and a div will not apply to me. When equal value declarations are made, the last one descending will take precedence.
